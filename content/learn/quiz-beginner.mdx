Five questions covering chapters 1–5. No time limit. Try to answer each one before you reveal it.

<Section n={2} title="Name this meld">

<TileRow tiles={[

]} gap="tight" />

Chow, pung, or kong?

<Callout title="Answer — Chow">
Three consecutive tiles in the same suit. Chows only work within a single suit — never across suits, and never with winds or dragons.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section n={3} title="Call priority">

Two players want the same discard simultaneously. Player A wants it to complete a pung. Player B wants it to win. Who gets the tile?

<Callout title="Answer — Player B">
Priority order: **winning hand beats everything** → kong or pung → chow. A win claim always outranks a pung, which always outranks a chow.
</Callout>

</Section>

---

<Section n={4} title="What is self-draw?">

A player draws the winning tile themselves from the wall instead of claiming someone's discard. What does this change about payment?

<Callout title="Answer — All three opponents pay">
Normally only the discarder pays the winner. Self-draw (自摸) means all three opponents each pay the full amount. It also adds +1 faan to the hand's score.
</Callout>

</Section>
