Mahjong is a four-player game played with tiles, not cards. It originated in nineteenth-century China, and the version played in Hong Kong — the version this site teaches — is the most widely-known traditional ruleset outside of mainland China.

The shape of a session is simple: shuffle, build, deal, take turns drawing and discarding tiles, and try to be the first to complete a winning hand. The vocabulary takes a few sittings to internalize, but the core rhythm of *draw, decide, discard* is something most people pick up in their first hand.

## How to use this site

There are three pillars, each linked from the home page.

- **Learn** — these chapters. Read them in order if you've never played, or skim what you need.
- **Practice** — an interactive walkthrough of how a hand is set up, from picking seats to the deal.
- **Glossary** — every tile, term, and call, with English and Chinese where it matters.

<Callout title="On the language">
The Chinese names for tiles and calls are part of the game. Most players, even English-speaking ones, use them. We'll show both throughout — you'll be using *pung* and *chow* by chapter five.
</Callout>
