<ChapterHeader n={4} plain="The recipe for a" italic="winning hand." />

This chapter is all you need to play your first game tonight. HK mahjong can be played as a **0-point game** — no scoring rules, no minimum faan floor. You just race to assemble the shape before anyone else does. Master the shape first. Points come later.

A winning hand is fourteen tiles organized into one specific shape: **four melds and one pair**. Every tile must belong to a group — nothing left over.

## The melds

A meld is a group of three tiles that satisfies one of two rules.

## The pair

Two identical tiles. Any tile works — suited numbers, winds, dragons. Your hand needs exactly one pair, and it can't be used as part of a meld.

## Playing toward the shape

While you're playing, you hold **13 tiles**. You're always one tile short of the complete shape. On the turn you draw or claim the tile that fills the last slot, you have 14 — and you declare a win.

Most of your decisions come down to one question: which tiles are closest to completing a meld? A pair of 5-dots is one tile away from a pung. A 4-dot and 6-dot is one tile away from a chow.

Reading left to right:

- **3–4–5 Dots** — complete chow. Done.
- **Green Dragon × 3** — complete pung. Done.
- **7–8 Bamboo** — needs a 6 or 9 Bamboo to complete the chow.
- **1–2 Character** — needs a 3 Character to complete the chow.
- **Red Dragon × 2** — your pair. Holds the slot.
- **9 Dot** — isolated, doesn't fit anywhere. Discard this.

After discarding the 9 Dot you're down to 12 tiles, two draws away from winning — one tile to complete each of the two open chows.

<Callout title="Flower tiles — just draw again">
You'll notice a set of flower and season tiles in the wall. If you draw one, set it face-up in front of you and draw a replacement tile from the back of the wall. That's all you need to know for now — flowers give you a free extra draw.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Edge cases later">
A few special hands break the four-melds-and-a-pair shape — *thirteen orphans*, *seven pairs* — and they're covered in a later chapter. For now, the standard shape is everything you need.
</Callout>
