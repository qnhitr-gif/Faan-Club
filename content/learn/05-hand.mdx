A winning mahjong hand is fourteen tiles, organized into a specific shape: **four sets and a pair**.

## The pair

Two identical tiles. Any tile in the set will do — a pair of 5-bamboo, a pair of east winds, a pair of red dragons. You only need one pair in your final hand.

## The sets

Each set is *three tiles*. There are two kinds.

### Pung (碰) — three of a kind

Three identical tiles. Always allowed.

### Chow (上) — three in a row

Three consecutive tiles in the *same suit*. 4-5-6 dots. 7-8-9 bamboo. Never across suits, and never with honors.

### Kong (槓) — four of a kind

A bonus set: all four copies of the same tile. A kong counts as one set, not two — but it requires you to draw a replacement tile, much like a flower. Kongs are scored generously.

## Putting it together

Four sets plus a pair = 4 × 3 + 2 = **14 tiles**. That's a winning hand, exactly. While you're playing, your hand is 13 tiles. On the turn you complete it — by drawing or claiming the right tile — it becomes 14, and you declare a win.

<Callout title="Edge cases later">
A handful of special hands break this shape — *thirteen orphans*, *seven pairs* — and we'll mention them in scoring. For now, four-and-a-pair is everything you need.
</Callout>
