When a player discards a tile, the tile sits face-up briefly. Before the next draw happens, any other player at the table can *call* — claim the tile to complete one of their sets.

There are three calls: **pung**, **chow**, and **kong**.

## Pung — claiming a triplet

If you have two of a tile and somebody discards the third, you can call *pung* and take the discarded tile. You expose all three face-up in front of you and must immediately discard one of your other tiles.

Anyone at the table can call pung — it doesn't matter whose turn was next. Pung beats chow if both are called on the same tile.

## Chow — claiming a sequence

If you can complete a three-in-a-row in the same suit using the discarded tile, you can call *chow*. You take the tile, expose the three together face-up, and discard.

  { suit: 'bamboo', value: 6 },

Chow has one restriction: **you can only chow from the player immediately before you in turn order** — the player on your right (since play goes counter-clockwise). If somebody across the table discards your missing 6-bamboo, you can't take it for a chow.

## Kong — claiming the fourth

If you already have three of a tile (a concealed triplet, or an exposed pung) and somebody discards the fourth, you can call *kong*. You expose all four, and then immediately draw a replacement tile from the back of the wall before discarding.

You can also kong from your own draw — if you have a triplet in your hand and you draw the fourth tile, you can declare a *concealed kong* on your turn.

## The trade-off

Calling exposes part of your hand to everyone. They can see what you're collecting and adjust their discards. A *concealed* hand — one with no calls — scores higher than the same hand with exposures. So even when a call is available, sometimes the right move is to skip it.

<Callout title="Priority">
If multiple players want to call on the same discarded tile, priority is: *winning hand* (game over!) → *kong / pung* → *chow*. Distance from the discarder doesn't matter for pung and kong; for chow, only the next player in turn order can call.
</Callout>
